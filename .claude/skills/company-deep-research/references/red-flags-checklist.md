# Red Flags Checklist

## Accounting Red Flags

### Revenue Recognition
- [ ] Revenue growing significantly faster than cash from operations
- [ ] Large gap between reported earnings and cash flow
- [ ] Aggressive revenue recognition policies
- [ ] Bill-and-hold arrangements
- [ ] Channel stuffing suspicions (end-of-quarter spikes)

### Balance Sheet Quality
- [ ] Accounts receivable growing faster than revenue (DSO increasing)
- [ ] Inventory growing faster than sales
- [ ] Unexplained goodwill increases
- [ ] Goodwill > 30% of total assets
- [ ] Frequent acquisition write-downs
- [ ] Rising intangible assets without clear value drivers

### Cash Flow Concerns
- [ ] Operating cash flow consistently below net income
- [ ] CapEx significantly lower than depreciation
- [ ] Working capital draining cash
- [ ] Heavy reliance on financing activities

### Audit Quality
- [ ] Auditor change (especially from Big 4 to smaller firm)
- [ ] Qualified audit opinion
- [ ] Material weakness in internal controls
- [ ] Late filings or restatements
- [ ] Going concern qualification

## Governance Red Flags

### Board & Management
- [ ] CEO also serving as Chairman
- [ ] Insider-dominated board
- [ ] Related party transactions
- [ ] Excessive executive compensation vs performance
- [ ] High management turnover (especially CFO)
- [ ] Key executives selling stock aggressively

### Ownership Structure
- [ ] Dual-class shares with concentrated voting power
- [ ] Complex holding structures
- [ ] VIE structures (China companies)
- [ ] Significant related-party ownership

### Disclosure Quality
- [ ] Vague or evasive answers in earnings calls
- [ ] Missing conference calls after results
- [ ] Complex or changing segment reporting
- [ ] Guidance frequently missed then revised

## Operational Red Flags

### Business Model
- [ ] Customer concentration (>20% from single customer)
- [ ] Supplier concentration risks
- [ ] Declining market share
- [ ] Obsolescence risk (technology shifts)
- [ ] Regulatory dependency

### Competitive Position
- [ ] Margins declining while competitors stable
- [ ] Losing key customers
- [ ] Unable to raise prices
- [ ] Increasing promotional activity
- [ ] Patent expirations without pipeline

### Capital Allocation
- [ ] Debt-funded buybacks at high valuations
- [ ] Dividend cuts or suspensions
- [ ] Dilutive acquisitions
- [ ] CapEx cuts below maintenance levels

## Financial Stress Indicators

### Liquidity
- [ ] Current ratio < 1
- [ ] Quick ratio declining
- [ ] Negative working capital
- [ ] Drawing on credit facilities
- [ ] Covenant violations or waivers

### Solvency
- [ ] Interest coverage < 3x
- [ ] Debt/EBITDA > 5x
- [ ] Rising refinancing risk
- [ ] Credit rating downgrades
- [ ] CDS spreads widening

## External Warning Signs

### Market Signals
- [ ] Short interest > 10% of float
- [ ] High-profile short seller reports
- [ ] Options market showing unusual activity
- [ ] Bond prices declining significantly

### Regulatory & Legal
- [ ] SEC inquiries or investigations
- [ ] DOJ subpoenas
- [ ] Whistleblower lawsuits
- [ ] Class action filings
- [ ] Regulatory fines or sanctions

### Media & Reputation
- [ ] Negative investigative journalism
- [ ] Former employee allegations
- [ ] Customer complaints trending up
- [ ] Social media sentiment deteriorating

## Due Diligence Response

When red flags are identified:

1. **Acknowledge explicitly** in the report
2. **Quantify impact** if possible
3. **Assess probability** of worst case
4. **Determine thesis impact** (fatal vs manageable)
5. **Define monitoring criteria** (what would make it worse/better)
